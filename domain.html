 Literature Survey

A comprehensive lierature review was done to gather information about previous research done in the field of Specific Learning Diasbilities specially Dyscalculia. A summary of the pros and cons of the researches when compared with each other and the CalcPal application is given below.

 Research Gap

Absence of a single solution that uniquely identifies for each variant of Dyscalcilia and provides level wise, interactive remedial activities for intervention accordingly. Additionally, there is also a lack of Speciifc Learning Disability related applications in Sinhala language with activities tailored to the Sri Lankan school syllabus.

 Research Problem

The research problem arises from several gaps found in existing studies on Dyscalculia screening and remediation. Firstly, there's a lack of a comprehensive solution that screens for all Dyscalculia subtypes. Additionally, there's a need for solutions available in both Sinhala and English, aligned with the Sri Lanka school syllabus. The absence of tailored interactive activities based on the specific Dyscalculia type students face is another issue. This can lead to students wasting time in doing activities for types of Dyscalculia they do not suffer from and missing the opportunity to attempt activities for the type of Dyscalculia they suffer from. Lastly, the oversight in considering general IQ during Dyscalculia screening indicates a broader gap in understanding the overall cognitive factors impacting mathematical difficulties. Addressing these gaps is crucial for developing an effective Dyscalculia screening and remediation tool.

 Research Objectives

Assist Primary school students suffering from Dyscalculia in a new and interactive way by,

  • 1
    Identifying the specific type of Dyscalculia students suffer from.
  • 2
    Providing relevant activities to improve their weak points.
  • 3
    Making learning math a more accessible and enjoyable experience for all students.

 Methodology

The system consists of 4 separate quizzes aimed at identifying 2 types of Dyscalculia each. The results from the 2 sections of the quiz will be collected and processed separately as shown in the diagram. The types of data resulting from the quizzes are different as portrayed. These results along with the IQ test score will be used to predict and screen for the subtype of dyscalculia. Following that according to the prediction results students will be directed to level wise interactive activities to improve their condition. The prediction results and activity results will be stored in the database and used to track the progress and generate reports

  • Methodology In Brief
  • 1> Medical expertise consultation to identify diagnosis question set for each Dyscalculia type.
  • 2> Dataset collection for risk identification of each Dyscalculia type
  • 3> Medical expertise consultation for the labelling of all the datasets.
  • 4> Model training using the best performing algorithms .
  • 5> Model testing and validation.
  • 6> Medical expertise and teacher consultation for identification of remedial activities.
  • 7> Development of frontend and backend services.
  • 8> Deployment of the backend services and models using Azure.
  • 9> Application testing.
